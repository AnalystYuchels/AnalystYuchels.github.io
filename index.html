What I Do

01

Full-Stack Web Development

I craft complete, end-to-end web applications tailored to your needs, covering every aspect from designing robust server-side APIs and managing relational databases to developing responsive, accessible, and user-friendly front-end interfaces. My expertise includes practical experience with Node.js, Express, PostgreSQL, EJS, HTML5, CSS3, and modern JavaScript (ES6+), along with RESTful API design, Git and GitHub workflows, MVC architecture, and secure authentication and session management. I’m committed to continuously expanding my skills and refining my approach through ongoing learning to deliver the best solutions for you.

02

Technical Writing

When it comes to crafting clear, well-structured documentation tailored to you, transforming complex systems into language that real people can easily understand and apply, I do a fantastic job. Whether it’s API documentation, user guides, or step-by-step tutorials, I prioritize clarity, flow, and practicality so both developers and end users can confidently navigate the tools and systems they depend on.

03

Technical Support

I approach troubleshooting with patience, empathy, and a methodical mindset that prioritises identifying root causes over quick fixes. I communicate findings and solutions clearly, tailoring my explanations for both technical teams and non-technical stakeholders to ensure everyone remains informed and aligned.

About

A bit about me

I'm Uchechi Uche, a full-stack web developer and technical writer based in Lagos, Nigeria. I am currently building my skills through project-based learning; I learn by building real, deployable applications rather than just following tutorials.

My background in technical support means I approach code the way I approach problems: methodically, with an interest in understanding the why before fixing the what. I believe good software and good good documentation are two sides of the same coin.

When I'm not writing code or documentation, I'm usually reading (fiction and non-fiction), learning something new, thinking about the next project to build, or simply just watching a movie.
